When people ask me what I'd do if I won the lottery, I've always said I'd donate the majority of it, sell all my things and walk the earth until my legs won't walk any more. After that I have no idea. Quite rightly, a few people have pointed out to me that you don't need to win the lottery to own nothing. I think this is somewhat true.

I grew up in a household where everything was kept, "It might be needed later", "It'll could come in handy", "You never know when you'll need it". "Just in case". "It's for a rainy day", and so on. It's ingrained in me to keep odds and ends for the future, reuse, reduce, recycle etc. Not owning odds and ends means you will eventually need to buy them for the full inflated price later on, and possibly at an inconvenient time.

That being said, needing to own things and owning things for the sake of it are two very different scenarios, and arguably, I don't _need_ to own things. I have no house to upkeep, no fence to keep painted, no car to maintain (besides my bus but I think that's a different beast) and so I probably don't need to worry about keeping odds and ends that I have nowhere to store.

Anyway, I've decided the few people who pointed this out are at least on the money that owning very little is something one can conceivably do. And so I've decided to try to reduce my possessions down to a goal and start walking. Partly because I've realised I don't like owning things, things take time and I'd rather do things than have things. Gandhi supposedly had ~ 10 possessions, a pretty incredible feat. I don't think I can quite get that low. I've lived before out of 2 bags for a few months, but they were heavy and I think much of that can be reduced.

So the goal is somewhere between 10 things and a carry on and under seat bag. As luck would have it a Serbian Businessman in an airport, who purported to be designing and making the first electric Ultra-car, told me about Wizz Airs unlimited fights club thing which allows you to fly a 3x a day every day all year (And I think it was a yearly fee of 400EUR) for ~ 10EUR a flight (the catch is that booking must be done < 3 days in advance). So my initial goal is 1 bag that fits under a Wizz Air seat. 40 x 30 x 20 cm in size and under 10Kg. Brilliant, we have a dimensional and weighted goal!

Below is my attempt at figuring out the minimum collection of things I can live with. I'm quite sure that, like after moving into the bus, I'll figure out ways to pack better and realise there's many things I simply don't use or need. I can already say with confidence my Windows Laptop will be a thorn in my side until I can get a thinner ARM one and share chargers with my MacBook.

I won't list things consumables like shampoo, toothpaste etc as those are not very interesting.

Below is the list with some commentary, and for thoroughness I've also organised it on [lighterpack](https://lighterpack.com/r/h8bzdv) with weights etc.

# The bag

## Bag
- Patagonia Black Hole
The bag is a little bigger than the under-seat size which is great for food shops. But it squishes down nicely. The bag also has some basic organisation which I find is done much better than any bag I've ever had. There are 2 sections, 1 for the laptop / tech and 1 for clothes etc. Both sections will zip off completely and are super nice. The bag can also be carried from almost any orientation and rearranged to be a shoulder bag which I find nicer when chaining public transit.

## Inner Clothing
- Darn Tough socks (x5)
- SmartWool Undies (x5)

I really can't oversell all of this Merino Wool stuff it works so much better for travelling with less items. It is easier to clean (e.g in a sink), it doesn't hold onto smells and it dries super quick. It might sound a bit TMI but if you want to reduce items this is a great start.

# How is it working out?

I've been living out of this bag, working little things, trying stuff out for about 2 months and I'm loving it. I know where everything is. Everything is organised. It's hard to lose track of things when I have so few. Packing for the next destination takes about 5 minutes and is easy to see everything is there! I was gifted a shirt for Christmas because my Mum can't resist. It's a nice shirt! And I'll donate one of my others. One in, one out.

Maybe the above reads like an insane take on life, but I find it brings me a lot of simplicity and peace.
